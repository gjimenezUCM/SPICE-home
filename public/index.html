About SPICE

SPICE (Social cohesion, Participation, and Inclusion through Cultural Engagement) is an EU Horizon 2020 funded project dedicated to citizen curation of cultural heritage. We are developing technologies that help communities select paintings, artefacts and other museum objects and share interpretations with others. Through this, citizens can learn more about themselves and develop a better understanding of, and empathy for, other communities. SPICE project defines an ecosystem of software services to support citizen curation. University Complutense of Madrid (UCM) group is in charge of two subsystems:

Community Model

Technological infrastructure to discover citizen communities based on similar characteristics and opinions when interacting with museum artefacts

VISIR

Visualization tool to assist curators to configure, visualize and compare different communities discoved by the community model

Community model

SPICE Community model is a Software as a Service that uses a selection of clustering algorithms that rely on similarity functions that use artefact attributes and citizen contributions to discover new citizen communities and enables visualization and explanation processes over explicit and implicit communities.

Explicit communities

Citizens belong to certain target groups (the static a priori categories) explicitly or according to their demographic attributes

Implicit communities

Inferred communities based on finding similar citizen contributions and/or with artworks with similar artefact attributes

VISIR

VISIR (VISualization for Interpretation and Reflection) is a visualization tool to assist museum curators in the analysis of communities, allowing reflection processes, and facing different points of view that can arise from citizen contributions, highlighting their similarities, differences, and relations.
